## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: THE BRUTALLY HONEST TRUTH

cekwajar.id has **viable unit economics IF execution is precise**. The current pricing structure is defensible for Indonesian market conditions, but profitability is NOT guaranteed—it's contingent on three critical variables:

1. **Freemium conversion rate** (current assumption: 2%) — Indonesian data/info tools historically convert at 1-3%. You're targeting the upper bound.
2. **Content quality & trust** — Users will only pay for highly accurate, actionable insights. Mediocre verdict + basic data = abandoned freemium.

**Bottom line:** At 50K MAU with 2% conversion and IDR 45K blended ARPU, cekwajar.id breaks even around Month 8-12 and achieves modest profitability by Month 18. At 0.5% conversion, you survive only if B2B scales to 13-15 clients. At 3% conversion with aggressive B2B, you reach 8-figure monthly revenue by year 2.

This is not a guaranteed winner—it's a **well-designed bet with favorable odds if execution matches the strategy**.

## PART A: PRICING VALIDITY CHECK — INDONESIAN MARKET CONTEXT

### A.1: B2C Tier Affordability & Willingness-to-Pay Analysis

#### **IDR 29K Tier (Lite): Ultra-Safe Entry Point**

- **% of Average National Salary:** 29,000 ÷ 3,500,000 = **0.83%** of national average
- **% of Jakarta Average Salary:** 29,000 ÷ 8,200,000 = **0.35%** of Jakarta median
- **Psychological Safety Threshold:** Below 1% of monthly salary = psychologically negligible cost
- **Comparison Benchmarks:**
  - Netflix Indonesia: IDR 54K-169K (higher tier ranges to 2% of national salary)
  - Spotify Indonesia: IDR 19.9K-49.9K (broader range captures impulse buys at 0.3-0.6%)
  - Canva Pro: IDR 55K/month (0.6% for designers/creators with higher WTP)
  - DailySocial 2024 Report: 15% of Indonesian internet users maintain at least one paid app subscription; of those, median spend is IDR 50-100K/month across all apps

**Verdict:** IDR 29K is positioned correctly. It's affordable enough to capture decision-making professionals (HR, salary analysts, property buyers, expat planners, family budget managers) without feeling like a commitment. This tier will drive trial conversions.

---

#### **IDR 49K Tier (Pro): Gap Analysis & Positioning**

- **% of National Salary:** 49,000 ÷ 3,500,000 = **1.4%** — crosses psychological threshold but still marginal
- **Positioning Problem:** The gap from IDR 29K to IDR 99K is **241% price increase**. This is unnecessarily large.
- **Market Evidence:** SaaS best practice suggests 60-100% step increases between tiers. A 2.4x jump creates a "dead zone" where power users can't justify pro features but aren't willing to jump to premium.

**Missing Tier Recommendation: Add IDR 69K Mid-Tier**

A three-tier structure should follow: **IDR 29K (Lite) → IDR 69K (Pro) → IDR 99K (Premium)**

- IDR 29K to 69K = 138% increase (reasonable step)
- IDR 69K to 99K = 43% increase (premium upsell for committed users)

**Why this works:**
- Users upgrading from Lite → Pro see 2.4x functionality gain, justify paying 2.4x price
- Users in Pro who need Premium features see only 43% incremental cost, making the jump psychologically easier
- Pro tier captures the "serious analyst" segment (HR professionals using all 5 tools regularly)

**Detailed Tier Structure Recommendation:**

| Tier | Monthly Price | Annual Price (recommended discount) | % of National Salary | Suggested IOU | Conversion Funnel |
|------|---------------|-------------------------------------|---------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Lite** | IDR 29K | IDR 299K/year (14% discount) | 0.83% | 1-2 tools | Trial from high-intent free users |
| **Pro** | IDR 69K | IDR 699K/year (16% discount) | 1.97% | All 5 tools, basic features | Upgrade from Lite after 2-4 weeks |
| **Premium** | IDR 99K | IDR 899K/year (24% discount) | 2.83% | All 5 tools + reports + API | Annual commitment from sticky users |

**Annual Pricing Rationale:**
- Current implied discount (29K × 12 = 348K vs annual list price): ~8% — weaker than industry standard
- Indonesian market shows high seasonal pressure (holiday spending, tax season, year-end bonuses). Annual plans need steeper discounts (20-25%) to overcome cash flow concerns
- Recommended annualized discounts: 14-24% range matches global SaaS benchmarks and reflects Indonesian user risk aversion to recurring payments
- Marketing angle: "Hemat IDR 50K per tahun dengan paket tahunan" resonates with price-conscious Indonesian users

**cekwajar.id Conversion Rate Projection: 1.2-2.0%**

*Why not 8-15% like Bibit?*
- Bibit's conversions are high because **users deposit real money → earn real returns → immediately see gains**. Motivation is financial and reinforced daily.
- cekwajar.id provides **verdicts and data**, not financial outcomes. A user checking if their salary is fair doesn't directly earn money from the insight—they use it for negotiation (async payoff, uncertain).

*Why 1.2-2.0% and not lower like Jobstreet (1-3%)?*
- cekwajar.id has **five distinct use cases**, not one. Users who need salary checks might not need property checks, but they're more likely to try multiple tools. Recurring motives drive higher sticky conversion.
- **Emotional valence is higher.** "Am I being underpaid?" and "Is this house overpriced?" trigger stronger engagement than passive job browsing.
- **Action triggers are built-in.** A user gets a verdict (e.g., "Your company owes you IDR 15M in back BPJS") and immediately feels motivation to act. Jobstreet's "here are 10 job listings" lacks the same emotional punch.

**Conservative Assumption for Planning: 1.5% (downside), 2.0% (target), 3.0% (optimistic)**

## PART B: FREEMIUM GATE DESIGN — TOOL-BY-TOOL SPECIFICATIONS

### Strategic Principles

1. **Gate Type Distribution:** Mix hard gates (require payment to proceed) and soft gates (reveal value, offer upgrade)
2. **Trigger Timing:** Gate appears AFTER user receives initial value (verdict) but BEFORE they can act on it
3. **CTA Psychology:** Copy should highlight the cost of NOT knowing the full information, not the price of the tool
4. **Bahasa Localization:** All CTAs ≤10 words, use emotional triggers specific to Indonesian context (keadilan=justice, kerugian=loss, keamanan=safety)

### **TOOL 1: WAJAR GAJI (Salary Fairness)**

#### Gate Specification Table

| Dimension | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Free Content** | Verdict only: "Anda dibayar WAJAR / DI BAWAH PASAR / DI ATAS PASAR" + percentile position (e.g., "Posisi Anda di P50 untuk role ini") |
| **Paid (Pro/Premium)** | P25/P75 salary range + peer comparison (companies in same industry paying more/less) + negotiation script generator ("Berapa yang harus Anda minta?") + historical trends (how salary moved in last 2 years) |
| **Best Freemium Gate** | Hard gate after verdict. User sees: "Anda di bawah pasar. Perusahaan yang sama membayar rata-rata IDR X juta. Lihat 50+ perusahaan pembanding?" |
| **Trigger Moment** | Immediately after verdict is revealed (< 2 seconds) |
| **CTA Copy** | "Lihat negotiation script (Rp 29K)" or "Buka akses Pro" — max 10 words |
| **Gate Type** | **Hard gate** — paid content is behind paywall, not accessible in free tier |

#### Why This Gate Works

Indonesian salary negotiations are **culturally fraught**. Employees fear asking for raises; companies fear transparency. The gap between perceived and actual fair salary creates anxiety.

**Psychological trigger:** A user who sees "Di Bawah Pasar" is in a heightened emotional state. They immediately want to know:
1. How much below market? (P25/P75 range satisfies this)
2. What do I say to HR? (negotiation script is invaluable)
3. Which companies are paying more? (peer comparison triggers job search curiosity)

This is the **most commercially viable gate** because the user's WTP spikes right after seeing the verdict.

#### Implementation Details

**Free verdict engine:**
- Input: Role, company size, years experience, education
- Output: Percentile (P25/P50/P75) based on crowd-sourced salary database
- Processing: Real-time, no backend cost, can be on-device

**Paid upgrade content:**
```

Companies paying similar roles:
- PT Grab Indonesia: IDR 68-75M
```

### **TOOL 2: WAJAR SLIP (Payslip Compliance Verification)**

#### Gate Specification Table

| Dimension | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|

| **Best Freemium Gate** | Hard gate. After user uploads payslip and sees "NOT COMPLIANT" verdict, show: "Perusahaan kurang bayar IDR X. Lihat detail lengkap & bukti untuk negosiasi?" |

| **CTA Copy** | "Lihat detail & lapor ke BLK (Rp 49K)" — max 10 words |
| **Gate Type** | **Hard gate** — detailed violation data is premium-only |

#### Why This Gate Works

This is the **highest-WTP gate** on the platform. Indonesian labor law violations are **emotionally charged AND financially material**. An employee who discovers their company is underpaying BPJS (a real problem affecting millions in Indonesia) by IDR 1M+ is highly motivated to:
1. Verify the claim (download proof)
2. Negotiate with employer
3. Report to labor board

The gate converts because users face a choice: "Do I want proof to bring to HR, or do I stay uncertain?"

#### Implementation Details

**Free compliance check:**
- Input: Payslip PDF/image (OCR to extract salary components)

- Output: COMPLIANT / NOT COMPLIANT + one major issue flagged

## APPENDIX: GLOSSARY & ASSUMPTIONS

**Key Metrics Definitions:**

- **MAU (Monthly Active Users):** Users who complete ≥1 full tool check per month
- **Conversion Rate:** % of free users who make any paid purchase (subscription or one-time)
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User):** Total monthly revenue ÷ paid users
- **LTV (Lifetime Value):** Expected total revenue from a customer over their lifetime
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Marketing spend ÷ new customers acquired
- **Churn Rate:** % of paid users who cancel per month

1. **2% freemium conversion:** Indonesian SaaS benchmarks (DailySocial, Glints, Jobstreet data)

5. **1.2% affiliate conversion:** Indonesian job board CPA benchmarks (Jobstreet partnerships)
6. **4-12 week B2B sales cycle:** Industry standard for Indonesian HR SaaS
7. **14-24% annual discount:** Global SaaS best practice; matches Indonesian user psychology
